PLATFORM

Our biosensing devices measure and stream in real time the electrodermal activity (skin sweating) and blood volume pulse (blood pulsations caused by the heart). The software gathers this data and extracts features related to nervous system activity, such as sudden palm sweating caused by the emotional events and heart rate variability, a metric influenced by relaxation and arousal. These features are then recorded for further analysis or streamed to any other systems, in shows and performances that usually is generation of audio or visual content or used to control the staging elements. This enables us to use physiological signals in real time as well as extract meaningful insights related to the user’s emotional or arousal state.

Corporeal Counterpoint

[Experimental Performances]

We used our system in a performance with a world renown performers. Audience’s biodata as well as their distance to the artist was picked by our system and streamed live to a eurorack setup to create the soundscape and spatialize it according to the position of the artist in the room. We focused on sonifying the liveness aspects of the performer and the audience, turning their heart beats and breathing into a musical soundscape. Also for this show we added room-scale positioning functionality to our sensing devices using BLE Beacon technology.

Living Photographs

[Photography & Biosensing]

We developed a new photography exhibition using our physiological sensing system to track the photographer's biometric signals when they take the photographs. Using a recording of the photographer’s heart, respiration, temperature and weather conditions in the moment they pressed the shutter, we create a multidimensional experience of a “living” photograph using audio, haptic, thermal and visual feedback to enhancing the emotional value of the piece and highlighting the human nature of the artist who took the picture.

Dance and Classical Music

[Augmented Performances]

Using our platform we enabled a series of classical music concerts and dance performances in Japan in collaboration with local artists. Music, lighting and projection mapping effects were generated in real time from the emotions of the audience. Our system recognized markers of emotions in the biodata of up to 70 spectators and that controlled the music and visuals.
